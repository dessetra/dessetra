# Sending & Receiving Crypto Safely

One of the most common mistakes beginners make in Web3 is sending digital assets incorrectly.

Unlike traditional banking, many blockchain transactions cannot be reversed.

This means a small mistake can result in permanent loss of funds.

## What Is A Wallet Address?

A wallet address is similar to an account number.

It tells the blockchain where digital assets should be sent.

A wallet address may look like:

```text
0x62c6e51faa933c02bb841bc4866840afbe50dbc8
```

Every character matters.

Sending funds to the wrong address can result in permanent loss.

## Sending Crypto

When sending crypto, always verify:

* The recipient address
* The amount
* The network

Never rush.

Always double-check before confirming a transaction.

## Receiving Crypto

When receiving crypto:

* Copy the address carefully
* Confirm the correct network
* Verify the asset being sent

If someone sends assets using the wrong network, the funds may not arrive correctly.

## Understanding Networks

A digital asset can exist on different networks.

For example, USDT may exist on:

* BEP20
* ERC20
* TRC20

The sender and receiver must use compatible networks.

If you are receiving USDT through BEP20, the sender must also send USDT through BEP20.

## Why Network Selection Matters

Choosing the wrong network is one of the most dangerous beginner mistakes.

For example:

If you give someone a BEP20 wallet address but they send using ERC20 or TRC20, the transaction may fail or the funds may become difficult to recover.

This is why Dessetra clearly states:

> Only use USDT BEP20 for wallet withdrawals.

## Gas Fees

Gas fees are small transaction fees paid to process blockchain transactions.

Different networks have different fees.

For example:

* Ethereum fees are often higher.
* BNB Smart Chain fees are usually lower.
* Tron fees may differ depending on platform conditions.

Before sending funds, always make sure you have enough balance to cover transaction fees.

## Safety Checklist Before Sending Crypto

Before confirming any transaction, ask:

* Is the wallet address correct?
* Is the network correct?
* Is the asset correct?
* Is the amount correct?
* Do I understand the transaction fee?

If any answer is uncertain, stop and verify first.

## Practical Example

If Dessetra asks for a USDT BEP20 wallet address, you should not submit:

* ERC20 address
* TRC20 address
* Bitcoin address
* Solana address
* Polygon address

You should only provide a wallet address that supports USDT on BEP20.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

Beginners often lose funds because they:

* Send to the wrong address
* Choose the wrong network
* Copy incomplete addresses
* Ignore transaction fees
* Trust someone else to verify for them
* Rush because of pressure

A smart Web3 participant slows down before confirming.

## Dessetra Insight

In Web3, speed can be dangerous.

Accuracy is more important than urgency.

Always verify before you send.
