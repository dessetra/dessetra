# Understanding Crypto Tokens

When many people first enter Web3, they hear words like:

* Bitcoin
* Ethereum
* USDT
* BNB
* Tokens
* Coins

At first, everything sounds the same.

But they are not the same.

Understanding the difference is one of the first steps to becoming a confident Web3 participant.

## What Is A Coin?

A coin is a digital asset that operates on its own blockchain.

Examples include:

* Bitcoin (BTC)
* Ethereum (ETH)
* BNB

These assets help power their respective networks.

For example:

Bitcoin is used on the Bitcoin network.

ETH is used on the Ethereum network.

BNB is used on the BNB Smart Chain.

## What Is A Token?

A token is a digital asset built on top of an existing blockchain.

Instead of creating its own blockchain, a token uses an existing network.

For example:

USDT can exist on:

* Ethereum
* BNB Smart Chain
* Tron

This makes USDT a token rather than a standalone coin.

## Different Types Of Tokens

### Utility Tokens

Utility tokens provide access to products or services.

They are designed to be used within a specific ecosystem.

### Governance Tokens

Governance tokens allow holders to participate in decision-making.

Owners may vote on upgrades, proposals, and community decisions.

### Stablecoins

Stablecoins are tokens designed to maintain a relatively stable value.

Examples include:

* USDT
* USDC

These are commonly used because they are easier to understand than highly volatile assets.

## Why This Matters

Many beginners buy assets without understanding what they are buying.

A smart Web3 participant asks:

* Is this a coin or a token?
* What is its purpose?
* What network does it use?
* What problem does it solve?

Understanding these basics helps you make better decisions and avoid costly mistakes.

## Dessetra Insight

In Web3, knowledge protects capital.

Before buying any digital asset, understand what it is, why it exists, and how it is used.
