# Avoiding Scams & Staying Safe

Web3 creates opportunities.

Unfortunately, it also attracts scammers.

Many people lose money not because blockchain failed, but because they trusted the wrong person, clicked the wrong link, or acted too quickly.

The good news is that most scams follow predictable patterns.

Learning to recognize them is one of the most valuable skills in Web3.

## Your Seed Phrase Is Your Wallet

When creating a wallet, you may receive:

* 12 words
* 24 words

These words are called a:

> Seed Phrase

Your seed phrase gives complete access to your wallet.

Anyone who obtains it can control your funds.

### Important Rule

Never share your seed phrase with:

* Friends
* Family
* Support agents
* Project administrators
* Anyone online

No legitimate platform will ever ask for your seed phrase.

## Fake Support Scams

One of the most common scams involves fake support agents.

Scammers may:

* Send direct messages
* Pretend to be customer support
* Claim your account has a problem
* Ask for passwords or recovery phrases

Remember:

Real support teams do not need your password or seed phrase.

## Giveaway Scams

You may see messages such as:

> Send 100 USDT and receive 200 USDT back.

Or:

> Limited-time investment opportunity with guaranteed profits.

These are classic scam tactics.

In Web3, there are no guaranteed profits.

If something sounds too good to be true, it usually is.

## Phishing Websites

Phishing websites are fake websites designed to look like legitimate platforms.

Their goal is to steal:

* Passwords
* Wallet credentials
* Seed phrases
* Personal information

Always:

* Check website URLs carefully
* Bookmark trusted websites
* Avoid clicking suspicious links

## Rug Pulls

A rug pull happens when a project attracts investors and then disappears with the funds.

Warning signs include:

* Anonymous teams
* Unrealistic promises
* No clear product
* Aggressive marketing
* Pressure to invest quickly

Before participating in any project, do your own research.

## Social Engineering

Some scammers do not attack technology.

They attack people.

They create urgency.

They create fear.

They create excitement.

Then they pressure you into making bad decisions.

A common tactic is:

> Act now or miss out forever.

A smart participant slows down and verifies information before acting.

## The Safety Mindset

The safest Web3 users are not necessarily the smartest.

They are the most disciplined.

They:

* Verify before trusting
* Research before investing
* Read before signing
* Think before acting

Security is not a one-time action.

It is a habit.

## Dessetra Insight

Opportunities will always exist in Web3.

Your first responsibility is protecting yourself.

A secure participant can always pursue future opportunities.

A careless participant may not get a second chance.
