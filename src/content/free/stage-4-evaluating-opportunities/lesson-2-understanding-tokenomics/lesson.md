# Understanding Tokenomics

One of the most important aspects of evaluating a Web3 project is understanding its tokenomics.

Tokenomics refers to the economic design of a token.

It helps answer questions such as:

- How many tokens exist?
- How are tokens distributed?
- What gives the token value?
- What role does the token play in the ecosystem?

## Why Tokenomics Matters

Many beginners focus only on price.

Experienced participants also examine supply and demand.

A token's long-term performance is often influenced by its economic structure.

## Token Supply

Projects usually define:

### Maximum Supply

The maximum number of tokens that can ever exist.

Example:

- Bitcoin has a maximum supply of 21 million coins.

### Circulating Supply

The number of tokens currently available in the market.

A project may have a large maximum supply but only a small circulating supply.

## Token Distribution

A good project should explain where tokens go.

Common allocations include:

- Community rewards
- Team allocations
- Development funds
- Ecosystem growth
- Investors

Transparency is important.

If token distribution is unclear, proceed carefully.

## Utility

A strong token often serves a purpose.

Examples include:

- Paying transaction fees
- Governance participation
- Accessing services
- Staking
- Ecosystem incentives

A token that serves no clear purpose may struggle to maintain value.

## Inflation And Emissions

Some projects continuously create new tokens.

This increases supply over time.

If demand does not grow alongside supply, token value may be affected.

This is why emission schedules are important.

## Questions To Ask

Before evaluating a token, consider:

- What is the maximum supply?
- What is the circulating supply?
- How are tokens distributed?
- What utility does the token have?
- Is token creation controlled?

Understanding these factors helps participants make more informed decisions.

## Dessetra Insight

Price is visible.

Tokenomics explains why the price behaves the way it does.

Strong decisions come from understanding the system behind the token rather than focusing only on short-term market movements.
