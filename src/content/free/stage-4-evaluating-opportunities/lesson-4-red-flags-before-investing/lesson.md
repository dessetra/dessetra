# Red Flags Before Investing

Not every opportunity deserves your attention.

One of the most valuable skills in Web3 is recognizing warning signs before committing money, time, or trust.

Many losses happen because people ignore obvious red flags.

## Guaranteed Profits

One of the most common warning signs is:

> Guaranteed returns.

No legitimate investment can guarantee profits.

Markets move.

Projects succeed and fail.

Risk always exists.

If someone promises:

- Guaranteed profits
- Risk-free returns
- Certain success

be extremely cautious.

## Pressure And Urgency

Scammers often create pressure.

Examples include:

- Buy now or miss out forever
- Only available today
- Last chance
- Limited time secret opportunity

Pressure reduces critical thinking.

Good opportunities can survive questions and research.

## Lack Of Transparency

Transparency matters.

Be careful if a project hides:

- Team information
- Token allocation
- Project documentation
- Product details
- Roadmaps

The less information available, the more cautious you should be.

## No Real Product

Some projects have:

- Big promises
- Large communities
- Aggressive marketing

But no product.

Always ask:

> What actually exists today?

A working product is usually more valuable than a long list of promises.

## Influencer Dependence

Some projects depend entirely on promotion.

Their message becomes:

- Buy now
- Price will increase
- Everyone is joining

Instead of:

- Here is the product
- Here is the utility
- Here is the problem we solve

Marketing is useful.

Utility is essential.

## Unrealistic Rewards

Extremely high rewards often indicate higher risk.

If an opportunity seems too good to be true:

- Slow down
- Research further
- Understand the risks

High rewards do not automatically mean high quality.

## Emotional Decision Making

Fear and excitement often create poor decisions.

Common emotional triggers include:

- FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)
- Greed
- Panic
- Social pressure

Successful participants learn to pause before acting.

## A Practical Safety Checklist

Before committing to any project:

- Do I understand it?
- Is there transparency?
- Is there a real product?
- Are rewards realistic?
- Am I being pressured?

If several warning signs appear at the same time, caution is often appropriate.

## Dessetra Insight

The goal is not to find opportunities quickly.

The goal is to avoid poor opportunities while identifying strong ones.

Protecting yourself from bad decisions is often more valuable than finding the next trend.
