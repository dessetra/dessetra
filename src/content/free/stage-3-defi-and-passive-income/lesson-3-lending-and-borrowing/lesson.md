# Lending & Borrowing In Web3

One of the most powerful features of DeFi is the ability to lend and borrow digital assets without using traditional banks.

Instead of relying on financial institutions, DeFi platforms use smart contracts to connect lenders and borrowers.

## How Lending Works

A lender deposits assets into a lending protocol.

These assets become available for other users to borrow.

In return, the lender may receive:

- Interest payments
- Platform rewards
- Incentives from the protocol

The lender earns because borrowers pay fees to access the funds.

## How Borrowing Works

Borrowers can access funds without selling their existing assets.

In many DeFi systems, borrowers must provide collateral.

Collateral is an asset pledged to secure the loan.

For example:

- Deposit ETH as collateral
- Borrow USDT against that collateral

This allows users to maintain ownership of their assets while accessing liquidity.

## Why People Borrow

Common reasons include:

- Accessing funds without selling assets
- Trading opportunities
- Business activities
- Portfolio management

However, borrowing introduces additional risks.

## Understanding Collateral

Most DeFi loans require collateral worth more than the amount borrowed.

This is called over-collateralization.

Example:

- Deposit $1,500 worth of ETH
- Borrow $1,000 worth of USDT

The extra collateral protects the protocol from market volatility.

## Liquidation Risk

One of the biggest risks in borrowing is liquidation.

If collateral value falls too much:

- Part of the collateral may be sold
- The loan position may be closed
- Losses may occur

This is why borrowers must monitor their positions carefully.

## Benefits Of Lending

Potential benefits include:

- Earning interest
- Generating passive income
- Participating in DeFi ecosystems

## Risks Of Lending

Potential risks include:

- Smart contract failures
- Platform exploits
- Market volatility
- Protocol insolvency

No lending platform is completely risk-free.

## Dessetra Insight

Lending and borrowing are useful financial tools.

However, every reward exists because risk exists.

Understanding collateral and liquidation is more important than chasing interest rates.
