# Liquidity Pools & Yield Farming

Many DeFi applications require liquidity to function.

Liquidity simply means assets are available for users to trade, swap, lend, or borrow.

Instead of relying on traditional market makers, many DeFi platforms use liquidity pools.

## What Is A Liquidity Pool?

A liquidity pool is a collection of digital assets locked inside a smart contract.

Users contribute assets to the pool.

These users are called:

> Liquidity Providers (LPs)

The pool allows other users to perform transactions without needing a traditional buyer and seller.

## Example

Imagine a pool containing:

- USDT
- ETH

Traders can swap between these assets.

Every swap generates fees.

A portion of those fees may be distributed to liquidity providers.

## Why People Provide Liquidity

People often provide liquidity to:

- Earn trading fees
- Receive protocol incentives
- Participate in DeFi ecosystems

Liquidity providers help the platform operate while potentially earning rewards.

## What Is Yield Farming?

Yield farming involves moving assets into DeFi systems to earn rewards.

This may include:

- Providing liquidity
- Staking LP tokens
- Participating in reward programs

The goal is often to maximize returns.

## Understanding Impermanent Loss

One of the most important risks of liquidity provision is:

> Impermanent Loss

This occurs when asset prices change significantly relative to each other.

In some situations, a liquidity provider may earn fees but still end up with less value than simply holding the assets.

This is one reason why high rewards should always be examined carefully.

## Benefits

Potential benefits include:

- Trading fee income
- Protocol rewards
- Ecosystem participation

## Risks

Potential risks include:

- Impermanent loss
- Smart contract vulnerabilities
- Platform exploits
- Market volatility

Higher rewards often indicate higher risks.

## Dessetra Insight

Liquidity and yield farming are powerful tools.

However, participants should understand where rewards come from and what risks they are accepting before contributing assets.
