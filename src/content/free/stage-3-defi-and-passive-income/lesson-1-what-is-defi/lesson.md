# What Is DeFi?

Most financial systems today depend on intermediaries.

Examples include:

- Banks
- Payment processors
- Loan providers
- Investment firms

These institutions help move money, approve transactions, and provide financial services.

DeFi stands for:

> Decentralized Finance

It is a collection of financial services built on blockchain networks.

Instead of relying completely on traditional institutions, users can interact with smart contracts.

## What Makes DeFi Different?

Traditional finance often requires:

- Bank accounts
- Approval processes
- Geographic restrictions
- Business hours

DeFi systems aim to provide:

- Global access
- 24/7 availability
- Transparent transactions
- Direct participation

Anyone with a compatible wallet and internet connection can usually interact with DeFi platforms.

## Examples Of DeFi Activities

People use DeFi to:

- Swap tokens
- Earn staking rewards
- Lend assets
- Borrow assets
- Provide liquidity

Each activity has different risks and potential rewards.

## Why DeFi Became Popular

Many people were attracted to DeFi because it introduced:

- Greater accessibility
- New earning opportunities
- Faster settlement systems
- Financial participation without traditional barriers

However, accessibility does not remove risk.

## Important Reality

DeFi is not free money.

Rewards usually exist because someone is taking risk, providing liquidity, or participating in a financial system.

Before seeking rewards, users must understand:

- How the system works
- What risks exist
- What could go wrong

Knowledge must come before participation.

## Dessetra Insight

The purpose of DeFi is not simply earning money.

Its broader purpose is creating financial systems that operate through transparent blockchain rules rather than relying entirely on centralized institutions.

Understanding the system is the first step toward using it responsibly
