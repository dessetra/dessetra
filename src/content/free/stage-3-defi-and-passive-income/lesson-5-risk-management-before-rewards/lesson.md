# Risk Management Before Rewards

One of the biggest mistakes beginners make in DeFi is focusing only on rewards.

Many opportunities advertise:

- High APY
- High staking rewards
- Large farming incentives
- Fast profits

Unfortunately, high rewards often come with high risk.

## What Is Risk Management?

Risk management means protecting your capital while pursuing opportunities.

Instead of asking:

> How much can I make?

Experienced participants first ask:

> What can I lose?

## Common DeFi Risks

### Smart Contract Risk

DeFi platforms rely on code.

If the code contains vulnerabilities, funds may be lost.

### Platform Risk

Even legitimate platforms can experience:

- Technical failures
- Security breaches
- Operational issues

### Market Risk

Asset prices can move quickly.

A position that appears profitable today may become unprofitable tomorrow.

### Liquidity Risk

Some assets may be difficult to sell when markets become stressed.

### Stablecoin Risk

Not all stablecoins are equally secure.

Some stablecoins have failed completely.

## Position Sizing

A common principle in investing is:

> Never risk more than you can afford to lose.

Participants often spread risk across multiple opportunities instead of concentrating everything in one place.

## Diversification

Diversification means avoiding dependence on a single asset or platform.

Examples include:

- Multiple assets
- Multiple protocols
- Multiple strategies

Diversification does not eliminate risk, but it may reduce exposure.

## Emotional Discipline

Many financial losses come from emotional decisions.

Common mistakes include:

- FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)
- Panic selling
- Chasing unrealistic returns
- Ignoring warning signs

The best decisions are usually made calmly and deliberately.

## A Practical Rule

Before entering any opportunity, ask:

- How does it work?
- Where do rewards come from?
- What are the risks?
- What happens if things go wrong?

If you cannot answer these questions, more learning may be needed before participating.

## Dessetra Insight

The objective is not simply earning rewards.

The objective is surviving long enough to benefit from opportunities over time.

Protecting capital is often more important than maximizing returns.
