# Understanding Staking

One of the first ways many people encounter DeFi is through staking.

Staking allows users to lock certain digital assets in a blockchain system and receive rewards for participating.

Think of staking as contributing assets to help support a network while earning rewards in return.

## Why Does Staking Exist?

Many blockchain networks need participants to help:

- Validate transactions
- Secure the network
- Maintain consensus

Instead of relying on expensive mining equipment, some blockchains use staking systems.

Participants who stake assets help the network operate.

In return, they may receive rewards.

## How Staking Rewards Work

When users stake assets:

1. Assets are locked or committed.
2. The blockchain uses those assets as part of its security system.
3. Rewards are distributed over time.

Rewards are usually expressed as an annual percentage rate.

However, rewards can change depending on:

- Network activity
- Number of participants
- Token economics

## Common Types Of Staking

### Native Staking

This involves staking directly on a blockchain network.

Examples include:

- Ethereum staking
- Solana staking
- Cardano staking

### Platform Staking

Some exchanges and platforms offer simplified staking services.

These services often make staking easier for beginners.

However, users should understand who controls the assets during the staking period.

## Benefits Of Staking

Potential advantages include:

- Earning rewards
- Supporting blockchain networks
- Long-term participation
- Passive income opportunities

## Risks Of Staking

Staking is not risk-free.

Possible risks include:

- Token price declines
- Lock-up periods
- Smart contract vulnerabilities
- Platform failures

A high reward does not automatically mean a good opportunity.

## Important Principle

Many beginners focus only on reward percentages.

Experienced participants also evaluate:

- Risk
- Security
- Liquidity
- Project quality

The reward is only one part of the decision.

## Dessetra Insight

Staking should be viewed as a financial decision, not a guarantee.

Understanding how rewards are generated is more important than chasing the highest percentage advertised.
